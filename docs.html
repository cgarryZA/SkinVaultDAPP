SkinVault Documentation

Introduction

SkinVault is a blockchain-based ETF-style tracker fund for Counter-Strike 2 (CS2) skins, designed to give everyone access to skin investing—whether you’re a gamer, trader, or crypto investor.
The value of this vault is tracked and accessible via SKINDEX, an Ethereum-based ERC-20 token that reflects the total net asset value (NAV) of the fund.

Why CS2 Skins? Skins are a unique, high-demand digital asset class with a strong secondary market, price appreciation history, and low correlation to broader crypto markets.

Tokenomics

SKINDEX is minted when you deposit Ethereum into the SkinVault SmartContract, at a rate determined by the current NAV and less applicable fees.

  • SKINDEX can be burned at any time to redeem a proportional share of the vault’s assets (ETH), minus the burn fee.
  • All minting and burning actions are transparently tracked on-chain—no supply pre-mined or reserved for founders.

SKINDEX Utility: SKINDEX can be held for value exposure, traded on DEXes/CEXes, or burned for ETH redemption.

Inflation/Deflation: Total SKINDEX supply only increases when new ETH is deposited and decreases when SKINDEX is burned for redemption.

System Architecture

The SmartContract manages multiple “Pools” of assets:

  • Skin Pool: Off-chain Steam accounts holding CS2 skins, managed by secure bots.
  • BTC Pool: Off-chain BTC reserves for added withdrawal liquidity.
  • ETH Pool: On-chain ETH reserves held in the contract for instant redemption/liquidity.
All pools combine to determine NAV. Vault value updates are based on market prices and third-party APIs.

Off-chain asset values (skins, BTC) are regularly synced by trusted oracles/bots. On-chain ETH is always visible.

Trading Bot Infrastructure

How it works: When deposits or withdrawals (burns) happen, the trading bot reviews vault inventory and market prices.

  • Under-represented skins trigger buy orders via 3rd-party market places.
  • Over-represented skins or excess value trigger sell orders.
  • Bots optimize for target portfolio allocation and execute trades automatically. Manual override is possible for upgrades or emergencies.
Platforms used: Steam Market, 3rd-party skin trading sites, and CEX/DEX for BTC/ETH conversion.

Valuation

The vault’s Net Asset Value (NAV) is calculated as the sum of:

  • ETH Pool (on-chain ETH balance)
  • BTC Pool (BTC reserves, valued at current price from a top-3 CEX)
  • Skin Pool (skin value from Pricempire API)

NAV is updated at least every 12 hours, or whenever large deposits/withdrawals happen.

Security Measures

  • Open-source smart contracts
  • Emergency pause/upgrade available to vault manager
  • External oracle for price feeds, monitored for manipulation

Key Risks Addressed: Vault cannot be rugged due to transparent, on-chain accounting and withdrawal logic. Steam accounts and API keys are secured and monitored. If off-chain assets become inaccessible, contract will pause withdrawals until value is restored.

Investment Rationale

CS2 skins have shown stable or appreciating value over time due to limited supply, high user demand, and strong gaming community support. By pooling assets and automating trading, SkinVault provides investors with easy, diversified exposure to this asset class, combined with crypto-native liquidity and transparency.

  • Historically, high-tier skins have outperformed many altcoins on a risk-adjusted basis.
  • Crypto and skins combined reduce correlation with traditional markets.

FAQs

  • Q: What prevents a rug pull?
    A: All assets are held in transparent, auditable smart contracts. No pre-mined SKINDEX exists and withdrawals are always matched by asset value.
  • Q: How do I deposit or withdraw?
    A: Connect your Ethereum wallet and use the Mint/Burn functions on our web app.
  • Q: What wallets are supported?
    A: MetaMask, with support for more wallets coming soon.
  • Q: How is my privacy protected?
    A: No personal info is collected unless required by law (AML/KYC for large withdrawals).
  • Q: What happens if the Steam accounts are banned?
    A: Trading is paused and a recovery process is initiated. NAV is protected; redemptions may be delayed.
  • Q: Can I lose money?
    A: Yes—crypto and skin prices can go down as well as up. Always do your own research.
  • Q: How often are values updated?
    A: At least every 12 hours, or more frequently if market conditions require.
  • Q: Where can I find the smart contract?
    A: SkinVault on Etherscan
  • Q: Who do I contact for support?
    A: See the Contact section below.

Glossary

  • NAV: Net Asset Value (total value of all pools in ETH terms)
  • SKINDEX: The ERC-20 token representing a share of the vault
  • Mint Fee: Fee taken when minting SKINDEX for ETH
  • Burn Fee: Fee taken when redeeming ETH for SKINDEX
  • Pool: Any asset collection tracked by the contract (ETH, BTC, Skins)
  • Pricempire: Leading price aggregator API for CS2 skins
  • Multisig: Wallet controlled by multiple independent parties
  • Oracles: Bots/scripts that provide off-chain price and inventory data to the contract
